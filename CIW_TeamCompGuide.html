Team Composition Guide


Team Composition or Building Introduction


Teams are essential when you want to become stronger in the game.
This guide will give you an idea on how teams and team building works in the game.
We will be tackling the main principles and basics about teams.
Keep mind that some teams are very flexible. The addition of new characters
and mechanics can change the rotations and usability of some teams.


A Good Team must have the following:



Good rotations

Good Energy Management

Good Reactions (Not needed for Mono Element Teams and Physical)

Good Survivability


Rotations

A good rotation must be able to fulfill each characters role efficiently.
The rotations can be planned for each character or the rotation can be flexible
wherein each characters can be swapped freely. Always keep in mind the cooldown of
each characters skill because they are essential in a proper rotation.

Energy

A good rotation must have good energy management. Energy is what characters use
in order to cast their elemental bursts. The Shimenawa's Reminiscence artifact set also
uses energy in order to utilize the 4-piece set. A DPS should have some energy recharge but should
not focus on energy recharge as its main stat (excluding Raiden Shogun). A support needs a good amount
of energy recharge because they need their elemental bursts to be usable when they are used in a rotation.
Some characters can act as a "battery" in order to give their team members or damage dealers
energy particles by switching to the character that needs energy while the energy particles
are going to the character. in order to cast their burst.

Reactions

Reactions work when an element is applied and an element triggers the element applied.
You can amplify the strength of reactions with elemental mastery. Some reactions either buff, debuff,
or does damage in general. The Vaporize reaction which uses hydro and pyro has a multiplier of 1.5x if the pyro
element is the trigger and a multiplier of 2x if the hydro element is the trigger. The Melt reaction which uses
cryo and pyro has a multiplier of 1.5x if the cryo element is the trigger and a multiplier of 2x if the pyro element
is the trigger. Reactions like Electro-Charged and Overload use the elemental mastery stat in order
for their damage. Electro-Charged uses hydro and electro while Overload uses pyro and electro.

These are damage dealing reactions that are seperate numbers from the character damage number.
These types of reactions are essential for some teams that use these reactions for their damage. Superconduct
is a reaction between cryo and electro. This reaction is to reduce to physical resistance of an enemy.
This is used for physical damage dealers. Swirl does damage with all elements besides Geo. It uses elemental mastery
and scales off your character's level. Crystallize creates elemental shields based on the applied element on the enemy.
Freeze is a reaction that happens when you apply cryo and hydro. Like the name says, you can freeze enemies but you
cannot freeze some enemies like bosses. Shatter happens when a frozen enemy is hit by a claymore or a heavy hit which
causes some damage. This reaction is not generally recommended because as of now it is weak.


Survivability

A good team needs to survive on the field of battle. You can either bring a healer or shielder or both.
Some characters can reduce the damage you take from enemies. They can reduce physical or elemental damage.
Some characters have iframes which are frames where characters cannot be hurt so learning your
character's iframe can be helpful. Some characters also have interruption resistance where they still take damage
but cannot knock you back. Knowing what characters provide the survivability you need can help your team
do more damage by not worrying if your character will die. This makes the rotations more comfortable.


Character Roles

You must learn the role of the characters you put in a team. You must learn if the character
you use is a damage dealer or dps, sub-dps, healer, shielder or damage reducer, and buffer.
You need to balance these in a team so your team can reach its full potential. Some characters
fulfill many roles while some fulfill one so learning what your character can do and
their strengths and weaknesses can create a better team for you to use when you are playing.



Now that you have learned the principles of a good team composition. You can use the
characters you have and try to draw out their full potential in a team you want to make.
Don't force yourself to use a character you don't like and always remember to have fun
in making a team and playing the game.
